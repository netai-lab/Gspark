Guided by Your Behavior: A Multimodal User-Behavior Dataset for Photorealistic Static and Dynamic Gaussian Splat Viewing

Anonymous Author(s) — Under Review for ACM Multimedia 2026

Figure 1: Illustration of downstream services and applications guided by multimodal cross-category user behavior analysis.

Abstract

The emerging 3D/4D Gaussian Splatting (GS) has revolutionized photorealistic virtualization, yet large-scale user behavior datasets for GS remain scarce. We introduce GSpark, the first large-scale multimodal user-behavior dataset for immersive GS viewing, covering diverse 3DGS (static) and 4DGS (dynamic) content. Using a commercial HMD, we recorded 6DoF motion and gaze signals from 81 participants across 12 high-fidelity scenes spanning four categories: garden-scale static, large-scale static, dynamic human avatars, and dynamic full scenes. We present a detailed acquisition system, dataset structure, and comprehensive cross-category analysis to reveal behavioral mechanisms. GSpark is publicly released to fuel research in volumetric streaming, compression, and XR optimization.

Acquisition System & Equipment

Our data acquisition prototype uses an HTC VIVE Focus 3 Vision HMD with dual eye-tracking cameras (90 Hz). The system integrates with Unity via OpenXR, communicating with a Python-based proxy renderer that supports heterogeneous GS representations (3DGS & 4DGS) via UDP. User 6DoF head poses (position + orientation) and gaze vectors (world-space quaternion) are recorded at 25–45 Hz depending on scene complexity. All GS models are rendered at original 1:1 scale to preserve geometric fidelity.

  • Hardware: NVIDIA RTX 3090 GPU + Intel i9-10900K, wireless HMD with swivel chair for free rotation & handheld controller for translation.
  • Software: Unity engine, custom UDP proxy, 4K4D [27] & 3DGS [11] renderers.
  • Recorded signals: Timestamp (ms), position (X,Y,Z), head rotation (quaternion/euler), gaze origin + orientation, convergence distance.

Participants & Procedure

We recruited 81 participants (age 19–56, 43 female, 38 male) with normal/corrected vision. Most had limited XR experience, yet reported high immersion (MOS > 4.2/5). Each participant viewed all 12 GS stimuli in randomized category order. The experiment comprised: (1) preparation & IPD calibration, (2) interactive viewing with free 6DoF exploration, (3) post-questionnaire (demographics, immersion, cybersickness). Behavioral data was automatically logged without interruption.

📁 Dataset Structure & Format

The dataset follows a hierarchical folder structure for easy navigation:

├── Stimuli/               # sample rendered images of each scene
├── Category_UserBehavior/
│   ├── Garden_3DGS/
│   │   ├── Garden_Outdoor/
│   │   │   ├── UID001_Garden.csv
│   │   │   └── ...
│   ├── LargeScale_3DGS/
│   ├── Dynamic_Human_4DGS/
│   └── Dynamic_FullScene_4DGS/
├── Readme.md              # acquisition parameters, coordinate system, equipment details
└── LICENSE.txt

Each CSV includes columns: Timestamp (ms), Scene_X/Y/Z (meters), Head_RotX/Y/Z (degrees), Eye_RotX/Y/Z/W (quaternion). Sampling rates vary between 25–45 Hz depending on scene rendering load. The README file provides comprehensive documentation on coordinate conventions and usage.
